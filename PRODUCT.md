# Product

## Register

product

## Users

CBSE-affiliated school teachers and content administrators use this tool during paper creation and review. Teachers need to assemble, inspect, edit, swap, approve, and export board-faithful Class 10 Science question papers without manually remembering the blueprint or formatting rules. Content administrators use the system to curate and verify the Question bank that powers generated papers.

## Product Purpose

Question Paper Generator helps a teacher start from a near-finished, CBSE-style draft instead of a blank document. It assembles a `PaperDocumentV1` from verified Questions, presents it in an editable review workspace, and exports final paper artifacts after teacher approval. Success means the teacher can trust the paper structure, marks, source fidelity, and final print formatting while still retaining full editorial control.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, trustworthy. The interface should feel like a professional exam-review workspace: quiet enough for long editing sessions, exact enough for marks and section structure, and familiar enough that teachers do not need to learn decorative UI patterns before doing their work.

## Anti-references

Do not make the editor feel like a marketing page, a playful AI writing tool, or a generic card-heavy SaaS dashboard. Avoid decorative gradients, oversized hero-style typography, novelty controls, and visual treatments that compete with the paper itself. The paper canvas should remain the main object of attention.

## Design Principles

- Make the paper the product: the editor chrome supports review, but the document surface must read like the final artifact.
- Preserve teacher trust: marks, numbering, section structure, source text, and edit state must be unambiguous.
- Prefer familiar controls: use standard buttons, rails, panels, focus states, and text editing affordances.
- Keep long sessions comfortable: restrained contrast, stable spacing, legible typography, and minimal visual noise.
- Surface uncertainty clearly: warnings, unfilled Slots, locked state, modified text, and alternatives should be visible without disrupting paper review.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for WCAG AA contrast for all app chrome and editable paper text. Preserve keyboard access to question selection, drag controls, editor regions, and action buttons. Respect reduced-motion preferences, and keep color-independent indicators for state such as selected, locked, modified, and warning.
